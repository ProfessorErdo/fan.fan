The Problem

The Limitation of Traditional PLC Frameworks

The Product Life Cycle (PLC) framework is a cornerstone of strategic marketing and product management. However, its practical utility is severely limited by a critical gap: the inability to accurately predict when products transition between stages.

Traditional approaches rely on manual analysis, historical analogies, or simple heuristics—methods that fail to capture the complex patterns in modern retail data.

  • ✕ Subjective assessments lead to inconsistent predictions
  • ✕ Lack of quantitative metrics for lifecycle stage transitions
  • ✕ Inability to process large-scale turnover data efficiently
  • ✕ No systematic way to identify seasonal vs. structural patterns
  • ✕ Reactive rather than proactive decision-making

The Solution

A Data-Driven Approach

End-to-end machine learning pipeline that transforms raw turnover data into actionable lifespan predictions

1

Pattern Recognition with DTW

Dynamic Time Warping measures similarity between product turnover curves, identifying products with similar lifecycle patterns regardless of timing variations

2

Seasonal Intelligence

Quantifies seasonal intensity using variance decomposition, distinguishing between seasonal products (ski equipment) and year-round performers (basic apparel)

3

Product Segmentation

K-means clustering groups products by turnover behavior, enabling the model to learn distinct lifecycle patterns for different product categories

4

Predictive Modeling

Gradient boosting models (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost) trained on engineered features predict lifespan with hyperparameter optimization via Optuna

5

Interactive Dashboard

Streamlit-based visualization tool enables business users to explore predictions, view turnover trends, and understand model reasoning

Implementation

Data Pipeline

Eight-stage processing pipeline from raw data to predictions

Step 0: Data Extraction

Load turnover data and product hierarchy from enterprise data warehouse (ODPS)

Step 1: DTW Distance Calculation

Compute Dynamic Time Warping distances to mean and standard turnover curves for pattern recognition

Step 2: Seasonal Intensity

Decompose time series and calculate intensity = var(seasonal) / var(raw), classify as weak/medium/strong

Step 3: K-means Clustering

Segment products using DTW distances and intensity metrics, determine optimal k via elbow analysis

Step 4: Feature Assembly

Merge all features, calculate lifespan from turnover curves, stratified split by cluster and typology

Step 5: Model Training

Train gradient boosting models with Optuna hyperparameter optimization and early stopping

Step 6: Prediction Generation

Apply best model to generate lifespan predictions for active products

Step 7: Dashboard Export

Prepare data for interactive Streamlit visualization with trend charts and feature breakdowns
